## Design Characteristics

### Typography
All templates use executive-appropriate serif fonts:
- **Georgia**: Professional, highly readable
- **Garamond**: Elegant, sophisticated
- **Times New Roman**: Classic, traditional
- **Baskerville**: Distinguished, refined

### Color Schemes
Professional executive color palettes:
- **Navy Blue** (#1e3a8a, #1e40af): Trust, authority, professionalism
- **Burgundy/Wine** (#7c2d12, #881337): Sophistication, prestige
- **Charcoal/Gray** (#262626, #374151): Modern, professional
- **Forest Green** (#14532d): Growth, stability
- **Gold Accents** (#d97706): Premium, excellence

### Layout Philosophy
- **Single-column (1-5)**: Traditional executive style, high ATS compatibility
- **Two-column (6-8)**: Modern executive with sidebar, good ATS compatibility
- **Premium PDF-only (9-10)**: Maximum visual impact, moderate ATS compatibility

## Best Practices

### Content Guidelines
1. **Executive Summary**: 3-5 impactful sentences
2. **Experience**: Focus on leadership and impact
3. **Metrics**: Include quantifiable achievements
4. **Board Positions**: List all current and past roles
5. **Education**: Highlight executive programs

### Formatting Tips
1. Keep to 2 pages maximum
2. Use bullet points for achievements
3. Lead with impact and results
4. Emphasize strategic initiatives
5. Include relevant board experience

### ATS Optimization
1. Use templates 1-5 for maximum ATS compatibility
2. Include relevant keywords naturally
3. Use standard section titles
4. Keep formatting simple in DOCX
5. Test with ATS scanning tools
